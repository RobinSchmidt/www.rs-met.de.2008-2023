On this page, you will find a collection of links to various books and papers related to audio signal processing and programming. These links all point to the original works outside this site, so if any of them seems to be broken by the time you click it, chances are that the original author moved or removed the link-target. In this case, I'd be grateful for a notification so I may update or remove the link. The order of the topics is roughly determined by what I consider as most relevant for my own work and the order of the links within each topic by what I consider to be a good progression from basic to more advanced material.

Signal Processing

Books

The Scientist and Engineer's Guide to Digital Signal Processing (by Steven W. Smith)
Introductory book on DSP.
Individual chapters can be viewed online and downloaded as pdf.

Mathematics of the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) with Audio Applications (by Julius O. Smith III)
Book about the discrete Fourier transform. Explains also the fundamentals of complex numbers and some general concepts in digital signal processing. Can be viewed online as html.

Introduction to Digital Filters with Audio Applications (by Julius O. Smith III)
Book about digital filters. Can be viewed online as html.

Physical Audio Signal Processing for Virtual Musical Instruments and Audio Effects (by Julius O. Smith III)
Covers a lot of topics that are relevant in the context of audio-specific DSP inlcuding interpolation, delaylines, reverberation, modulation-effects, physical modeling, etc. Can be viewed online as html.

Spectral Audio Signal Processing (by Julius O. Smith III)
A book about modeling signals with sinusoids and/or noise and about the STFT time-frequency represenation. Can be viewed online as html.

Introduction to Signal Processing (by Sophocles Orfanidis)
General DSP textbook.
Available as pdf download .
Additional material: Solution manual , MatLab functions , C functions ,

Optimum Signal Processing (by Sophocles Orfanidis)
Textbook on optimum signal processing (statistical signal models, adaptive filters, etc.).
Available as pdf download

Selected Papers

Reverb

A reverberator based on absorbent all-pass filters (by Luke Dahl, Jean-Marc Jot)
Extends the general FDN reverb structrue by putting allpass filters in series to the delaylines.

Filters

Lecture Notes on Elliptic Filter Design (by Sophocles Orfanidis)
Quite comprehensive paper on elliptic filter design, covering also Chebychev I and II and Butterworth filters as special cases.
Additional material: MatLab files

High-Order Digital Parametric Equalizer Design (by Sophocles Orfanidis)
Extends classical textbook lowpass prototype filter designs (Butterworth, Chebychev I and II, elliptic) to the low-shelving case which also results in algorithms for high-shelving and peaking designs via lowpass-to-lowpass and lowpass-to-bandpass transforms.
Additional material: MatLab files

Websites

Relating Tuning and Timbre (by William Sethares)
A short summary of the main ideas in William Sethares' book "Tuning, Timbre, Spectrum, Scale", where he provides the foundations for a psychoacoustically motivated unifying framework for music theory. Within this framework, classical western music theory appears as the special case, where we consider only a specific subset of all possible timbres - namely those, where the partials are approximately harmonics of some fundamental frequency. This book is a very profound revelation and a must-read for every music theorist.
